Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about our real estate services

How long does the home buying process typically take?

The home buying process typically takes 30-45 days from offer acceptance to closing, but this can vary based on factors like financing, inspections, and market conditions. We work efficiently to streamline the process while ensuring all details are properly addressed.

What are the current market trends in our area?

Market trends vary by neighborhood and property type. Currently, we're seeing strong demand for single-family homes with outdoor space, while condominium markets are experiencing slight price adjustments. Contact us for a personalized market analysis specific to your property or desired area.

How much should I budget for closing costs?

Closing costs typically range from 2% to 5% of the home's purchase price. These include loan origination fees, appraisal costs, title insurance, and prepaid items like property taxes and homeowner's insurance. We provide a detailed estimate during the offer process so you can plan accordingly.

Do I need a home inspection, and what does it cover?

Yes, we strongly recommend a professional home inspection. A standard inspection covers the foundation, roof, plumbing, electrical systems, HVAC, and major appliances. Additional specialized inspections may be needed for pests, radon, or structural issues depending on the property.

What's the difference between pre-qualification and pre-approval?

Pre-qualification is a preliminary assessment based on unverified information you provide, while pre-approval involves a thorough financial check by a lender. Sellers typically prefer buyers with pre-approval as it demonstrates serious buying capability and can strengthen your offer in competitive situations.

How do you determine the right listing price for my home?

We conduct a comprehensive comparative market analysis (CMA) that examines recently sold properties with similar features in your area, current market conditions, and unique aspects of your home. This data-driven approach helps us recommend a pricing strategy that maximizes your return while attracting qualified buyers.
